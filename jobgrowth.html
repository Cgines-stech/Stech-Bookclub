Simplified Comparison

A smaller way to think about the same job growth story.

One simple way to understand the data is this: when you combine all industries that lost jobs and compare them to the industries that gained jobs, Iron County gained about 4.6 jobs for every 1 job lost. Rounded into plain language, that is about 5 gained for every 1 lost.

Jobs gained: 1,562
Jobs lost: 340
Simplified ratio: about 5 to 1
Net growth: +1,222

Source: Cedar City Economic Data
Notable Statements from website:
    The construction industry was the standout performer, adding 963 jobs and growing at a state-leading rate of 38.8%.
    Government remains the largest employment sector, currently providing 25% of the county's total jobs.
    Iron County's average monthly wage sits at $4,110, following an annual increase of 3.8%.
    Wages in the information and construction sectors led the growth, increasing by 23% and 17% respectively.
